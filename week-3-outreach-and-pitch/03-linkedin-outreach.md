# Module 3.3 — LinkedIn Outreach

**Reading Time:** 18 minutes
**Objective:** Build a LinkedIn outreach system that generates qualified conversations — from profile optimization to message templates to content strategy.

---

## LinkedIn Is Your Digital Handshake

Before a prospect ever gets on a call with you, they're going to look you up. And in B2B sales, that means LinkedIn.

Your LinkedIn profile is not a resume. It's a landing page. Every element — headline, banner, about section, featured posts — should answer one question for the visitor: **"Can this person help me?"**

If your profile doesn't answer that clearly, your outreach messages won't work. People check profiles before they reply. A weak profile kills strong messages.

## Profile Optimization (Do This Before You Send a Single Message)

### Headline

Your headline is the first thing people see in connection requests, comments, and search results. Make it count.

**Bad:** "Sales Representative at Nichefinders AI"
**Bad:** "AI Enthusiast | Business Development | Growth Hacker"
**Good:** "Helping service businesses automate operations with AI | Nichefinders AI"
**Better:** "I help $1-10M businesses get 20+ hours/week back with AI | Nichefinders AI"

The formula: **[What you do] + [for whom] + [result] | [Company]**

- Professional but approachable. Suit and tie is fine. So is a clean polo.
- Clear face, good lighting, simple background.
- Smiling. Not smirking, not stone-faced. A real smile.
- No group photos, no sunglasses, no distance shots.

## The Three Outreach Templates

Every outreach message should follow the **3-C Framework:**
1. **Context** — Why are you reaching out to THIS person?
2. **Credibility** — Why should they listen to you?
3. **CTA** — What do you want them to do next?

Messages without all three get ignored. Context without a CTA is a compliment. A CTA without context is spam.

### Template 1 — Warm Outreach (They Posted Something Relevant)

This is your highest-converting template. You're responding to something they actually said, which makes the message feel personal and relevant.

> Hi [Name],
>
> Saw your post about [topic — be specific: "hiring challenges," "trying to keep up with social media," "your new location opening"]. That's exactly the kind of challenge we help business owners solve.
>
> We build AI operating systems — think of it as a digital chief of staff that handles your content, data, meetings, and repetitive tasks so you can focus on growth.
>
> Our clients typically get 20+ hours/week back within 90 days. One of our partners — a service company with 3 locations — went from inconsistent posting to 9 pieces of content per week, fully automated.
>
> Worth a 20-minute call to see if something like that could work for [Business Name]?
>
> — [Your Name], Nichefinders AI

**Why this works:**
- Opens with something THEY said — shows you're paying attention
- Bridges from their pain to your solution naturally
- Includes a specific result (not vague "we help businesses")
- Asks for 20 minutes, not "a meeting" — small commitment

**Before sending:** Make sure you actually engage with their post first. Like it. Leave a thoughtful comment. Then send the DM 24-48 hours later. Cold DM after zero engagement looks automated.

### Template 2 — Cold Outreach (No Prior Interaction)

Use this for prospects you've researched but haven't engaged with yet. The key is a **specific observation** about their business that proves you did your homework.

> Hi [Name],
>
> I came across [Business Name] and noticed [specific observation — examples below]. We work with businesses your size that are ready to leverage AI — not just ChatGPT, but a full operating system that runs the day-to-day.
>
> The quick version: we help owners get back 20+ hours/week by automating follow-ups, content, reporting, and operations. Our Architecture phase is $15K and you see the full blueprint before committing to anything else.
>
> Would love to show you what that looks like. Quick call this week?
>
> — [Your Name], Nichefinders AI

**Specific observation examples:**
- "I noticed you've got great reviews but your social media hasn't been updated in a few weeks"
- "I saw you recently opened a second location — congrats. That usually comes with a lot of operational complexity"
- "Your website mentions you're hiring — that tells me you're growing fast, which usually means systems need to catch up"
- "I noticed your Google Business Profile could use some attention — that's low-hanging fruit that most businesses miss"

**Never use:**
- "I came across your profile and was impressed" (generic, sounds automated)
- "I'd love to pick your brain" (vague, no value)
- "I think we could really help you" (presumptuous without context)

### Template 3 — Application Response (2-Hour SLA)

When someone applies through aios.nichefinders.ai, they've raised their hand. Speed matters — respond within 2 hours during business hours. This is your warmest lead.

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for applying to the AI OS Partner Program. I looked into [Business Name] and I can see some immediate opportunities — especially around [reference something from their application: "your lead follow-up process," "content creation bottleneck," "team communication"].
>
> Our CEO Corey Reeder works directly with every new partner. He'd love to spend 45 minutes learning about your business and showing you what the AI OS could look like for [Business Name].
>
> Here's his calendar: [Calendly/booking link]
>
> Looking forward to it.
>
> — [Your Name], Nichefinders AI

**Key details:**
- Reference their application specifically — proves you read it
- Mention Corey by name — positions the CEO-level involvement as a benefit
- 45 minutes — matches the discovery call structure
- Direct calendar link — remove friction. Don't say "when works for you?" — give them a link.

## The Follow-Up Sequence

Most deals don't close on the first message. They close on the follow-up. Here's the LinkedIn follow-up cadence:

### Day 5 — Bump with Added Value (No Reply)

> Hey [Name], wanted to bump this in case it got buried. I actually just put together a quick breakdown of how AI is being used by [their industry] companies to cut admin time in half. Happy to share if you're interested — no strings.

**Why this works:** You're not saying "did you see my message?" (needy). You're adding value and giving them a reason to re-engage.

### Day 12 — Final Touch (Still No Reply)

> Hi [Name], last message from me — I don't want to be that person clogging your inbox. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. If things change down the road, you know where to find me. Wishing you and [Business Name] a great [quarter/season].

**Why this works:** You're explicitly saying "I'm backing off," which paradoxically makes some people respond. It removes all pressure and leaves a positive impression.

### The Rule of Three

**Never send more than 3 LinkedIn messages without a response.** After 3 unanswered messages:
- Move them to the nurture list
- Continue engaging with their content (likes, comments) if they post
- Check back in 90 days with a new angle

Desperation kills deals. If you send 5, 6, 7 follow-ups, you're not persistent — you're annoying. And you've burned a prospect who might have been ready in 6 months.

## Connection Request Strategy

Before you can DM someone, you usually need to connect. Your connection request is your first impression.

### The Note Matters

Always send a note with your request. Blank connection requests get accepted at lower rates and feel impersonal.

**Good connection request note (300 characters max):**

> Hi [Name] — I work with [industry] business owners to implement AI systems that save 20+ hours/week. Saw [Business Name] and thought we might have some common ground. Would love to connect.

**For referrals:**

> Hi [Name] — [Referrer Name] suggested we connect. I work with [industry] companies on AI operations. Would love to be in your network.

### Who to Connect With

Focus your connection requests on:

| Priority | Who | Why |
|----------|-----|-----|
| 1 | Business owners $1-10M | Your buyers |
| 2 | Decision makers (COO, VP Ops, GM) | Your influencers |
| 3 | Referral partners (accountants, lawyers, coaches) | They know your buyers |
| 4 | Industry peers | Learning + credibility |

**Daily connection target:** 10-15 targeted connection requests per day. Not random — researched and intentional.

## Content Strategy (Supporting Outreach)

Cold outreach without content is like cold calling without a voicemail — you get one shot and no reinforcement. Content creates familiarity. When someone sees your name in their DMs AND in their feed, you're not a stranger. You're someone they recognize.

### Posting Frequency

**Minimum:** 3 posts per week
**Ideal:** 5 posts per week (weekdays)
**Format mix:** 2 text posts, 1 carousel or image, 1 story/insight, 1 engagement post (question or poll)

### Content Pillars

Structure your content around these four pillars:

**1. AI Insights (Educational)**
- "Here's how AI is changing [industry] in 2026"
- "3 things most businesses get wrong about AI automation"
- "The difference between using AI and having an AI system"
- Purpose: Position yourself as an expert

**2. Client Results (Social Proof)**
- "One of our clients went from 4 blog posts/month to 36 — without hiring anyone"
- "A service company got 20+ hours/week back in 90 days. Here's how."
- Always anonymize unless you have explicit permission to name the client
- Purpose: Prove you deliver results

**3. Business Automation Tips (Value)**
- "5 tasks you're doing manually that AI can handle today"
- "The #1 reason your follow-ups slip through the cracks"
- "How to audit your business for automation opportunities"
- Purpose: Give actionable value that builds trust

**4. Behind the Scenes (Personal Brand)**
- "Here's what a typical day looks like building AI systems"
- "Lesson I learned this week working with a $3M business"
- "Why I got into AI automation (it's not what you think)"
- Purpose: Make you relatable and human

### The Content-to-Outreach Flywheel

Here's how content and outreach work together:

1. You post valuable content consistently
2. Prospects see your posts in their feed (even before you connect)
3. They recognize your name when your connection request arrives
4. They accept because you're "that AI person" who posts useful stuff
5. You send a DM that feels natural — not cold
6. They respond because they already feel like they know you
7. The DM converts to a call

**SALES PSYCHOLOGY:** Reciprocity is the most powerful principle in sales. When you give value freely through content — tips, insights, case studies — people feel a subconscious obligation to reciprocate. That reciprocity shows up as openness to your outreach.

### Engagement Before Outreach

Before reaching out to a prospect:

1. **Day 1:** Like 2-3 of their recent posts
2. **Day 3:** Leave a thoughtful comment on one of their posts (not "Great post!" — something substantive)
3. **Day 5:** Send connection request with personalized note
4. **Day 7 (after connection):** Send your outreach DM

This 7-day warm-up turns a cold DM into a warm conversation. They've seen your name, you've engaged with their content, and now your message feels like a natural next step — not a random pitch from a stranger.

## LinkedIn DM Etiquette

### Do:
- Personalize every message (reference their business, post, or industry)
- Keep messages under 150 words for first touch
- Include a clear CTA (call, calendar link, or question)
- Respond to replies within 4 hours during business hours
- Use their first name
- Be direct about what you do — don't be mysterious

### Don't:
- Send the same template to everyone (they'll notice)
- Pitch in the connection request itself
- Send voice messages to people you don't know
- Follow up more than 3 times without a response
- Use "I" more than "you" in any message
- Send messages at 11pm (LinkedIn shows timestamps)
- Use emojis excessively
- Write a novel — if it requires scrolling, it's too long

### Response Time SLA

| Lead Source | Max Response Time |
|------------|------------------|
| Application (aios.nichefinders.ai) | 2 hours |
| Inbound DM (they messaged you first) | 4 hours |
| Reply to your outreach | Same business day |
| Referral introduction | 2 hours |
| Comment asking for more info | 4 hours |

Speed signals professionalism. Every hour you wait, the lead gets colder.

## Tracking and Metrics

Log all LinkedIn outreach in Airtable CRM. Track:

| Metric | Weekly Target |
|--------|-------------|
| Connection requests sent | 50-75 |
| Connection acceptance rate | 30-40% |
| DMs sent | 20-30 |
| DM response rate | 15-25% |
| Calls booked from LinkedIn | 2-4 |
| Content posts published | 3-5 |
| Content engagement rate | 2-5% |

### Review Cycle

Every Friday, review your LinkedIn metrics:
- Which messages got responses? What did they have in common?
- Which content posts performed best? Double down on that format.
- Which prospects opened but didn't reply? Adjust your follow-up.
- What's your connection-to-conversation conversion rate?

This data tells you what's working. Without it, you're guessing.

## Key Takeaway

LinkedIn outreach is not about volume — it's about relevance. A single personalized message to a well-researched prospect will outperform 50 generic copy-paste DMs every time. Optimize your profile first, post content consistently, warm up your prospects before messaging, and never send more than 3 follow-ups without a response. The goal isn't to sell in a DM — it's to earn a conversation.
