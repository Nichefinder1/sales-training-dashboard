## Section 1: Sales Psychology Frameworks (Questions 1-5)

### Question 1
In SPIN Selling, what type of question creates urgency by revealing the true cost of a problem?

- A) Situation
- B) Problem
- C) Implication ✓
- D) Need-payoff

**Why this matters:** Implication questions are where deals are won. "What does it cost you every time a lead falls through the cracks?" forces the prospect to quantify the pain. Until they feel the weight of the number, there's no urgency to act. Most salespeople skip straight from Problem to Need-payoff and wonder why prospects say "I'll think about it."

---

### Question 2
In the Challenger Sale framework, what is the primary role of the salesperson?

- A) Build a deep personal relationship with the buyer
- B) Teach the prospect something they don't know, tailor the message, and guide the conversation ✓
- C) Ask as many discovery questions as possible
- D) Present the lowest-risk option and let the buyer decide

**Why this matters:** With AI OS, your prospects don't know what they don't know about AI. You can't wait for them to figure it out — you have to lead with insight. "Did you know the average contractor loses $50K/year in missed follow-ups?" is a Challenger move. It reframes their reality and positions you as the expert.

---

### Question 3
According to Cialdini, which principle explains why giving a free audit before a sales call increases the likelihood of a prospect engaging?

- A) Authority
- B) Consistency
- C) Reciprocity ✓
- D) Scarcity

**Why this matters:** When you give genuine value before asking for anything, the prospect feels a natural pull to reciprocate. A free tech audit or insight report costs you an hour but creates an obligation that a cold pitch never does. This is why top salespeople lead with value, not features.

---

### Question 4
Chris Voss's "mirroring" technique involves:

- A) Copying the prospect's body language
- B) Repeating the prospect's last 1-3 words as a question ✓
- C) Reflecting their emotions back to them verbally
- D) Matching their communication style (email vs. call)

**Why this matters:** Mirroring is the simplest technique in this entire program and one of the most effective. Prospect says: "We've been struggling with admin work." You say: "Admin work?" And then they give you a 3-minute monologue about every pain point in their business. Two words did what 10 discovery questions couldn't.

---

### Question 5
Loss aversion says that people feel losses approximately how much more intensely than equivalent gains?

- A) 50% more
- B) Equal intensity
- C) 2x more intensely ✓
- D) 5x more intensely

**Why this matters:** This changes how you frame everything. "You'll save $5K/month" is weak. "You're losing $5K every month right now" hits twice as hard — literally. Always frame the cost of inaction before you frame the benefit of action. The fear of continued loss is a stronger motivator than the hope of future gain.

## Section 2: Ideal Customer Profile (Questions 6-10)

### Question 6
What is the ideal revenue range for an AI OS prospect?

- A) Under $500K
- B) $500K - $1M
- C) $1M - $10M ✓
- D) Over $25M

**Why this matters:** Below $1M, the budget is too tight and the volume too low for AI OS ROI to materialize. Above $10-25M, you start encountering enterprise buying behavior (committees, long cycles). The $1M-$10M owner has the pain, the budget, and the authority to decide. Know your lane.

---

### Question 7
What is the "Operator Trap"?

- A) When a business owner over-hires and can't control costs
- B) When a business owner is so busy working IN the business they can't work ON it ✓
- C) When a business focuses on operations instead of marketing
- D) When an owner delegates too much and loses control

**Why this matters:** 80% of your prospects are in this trap. They know they need systems. They know they need to change. But they're too busy fighting today's fires to fix the root cause. Understanding this trap — and naming it for the prospect — is one of the most powerful things you can do on a discovery call.

---

### Question 8
Which industry is ranked as the BEST fit (Tier 1) for AI OS?

- A) Restaurants
- B) E-commerce
- C) Home services contractors ✓
- D) Manufacturing

**Why this matters:** Home services contractors (plumbing, HVAC, roofing, electrical) have high call volume, field teams, scheduling complexity, and owners deep in the Operator Trap. Fox Valley Plumbing & Backflow proves the model. When you know your best-fit industry, you can specialize your messaging, your outreach, and your case studies for maximum impact.

---

### Question 9
What is the ideal company size sweet spot for AI OS prospects?

- A) 1-4 employees
- B) 10-30 employees ✓
- C) 50-100 employees
- D) 200+ employees

**Why this matters:** 10-30 employees means the business is big enough to have real operational pain (scheduling, communication, follow-up) but small enough that the owner is still the sole decision-maker. No committees, no 6-month evaluation cycles. The person who feels the pain is the person who writes the check.

---

### Question 10
Which of the following is a LinkedIn signal that indicates a HIGH-priority prospect?

- A) "Just hit our 10-year anniversary!"
- B) "Trying to scale but I'm the bottleneck" ✓
- C) "We're hiring for a new receptionist"
- D) "Great Q1 results for the team"

**Why this matters:** "I'm the bottleneck" is practically a neon sign saying "I need the AI OS." This person is conscious of the Operator Trap, frustrated by it, and publicly acknowledging it. They're signaling urgency. When you see this, reach out within 48 hours with a value-add message — not a pitch.

## Section 3: Pain-to-Solution Mapping (Questions 11-15)

### Question 11
A prospect says: "We miss a lot of calls and people don't leave voicemails anymore." What AI OS module directly addresses this?

- A) AI Content Pipeline
- B) AI Call Answering + Automated Lead Capture ✓
- C) AI Dashboard + Reporting
- D) Internal Knowledge Base

**Why this matters:** Mapping pain to solution is the core skill of the AI OS salesperson. You don't explain the product catalog — you hear a specific problem and connect it to a specific solution. Missed calls = AI Call Answering. The prospect doesn't care about your feature list. They care that their phone stops going to voicemail.

---

### Question 12
When a prospect says "We tried ChatGPT and it didn't work," the best response is:

- A) "ChatGPT is actually a really powerful tool if you use it correctly."
- B) "Our AI is better than ChatGPT."
- C) "ChatGPT is a tool — like a hammer. What we build is the entire house." ✓
- D) "I'm sorry to hear that. Let's move on to discuss our product."

**Why this matters:** The tool-vs-system analogy is critical for overcoming the "we tried AI" objection. Most SMB owners equate "AI" with "ChatGPT" because that's their only reference point. Reframing from tool to system helps them understand that what we build is fundamentally different — integrated, automated, and designed for their specific business.

---

### Question 13
What is the "golden rule" of pain-to-solution mapping?

- A) Always present the most expensive solution first
- B) Match every pain to at least two modules
- C) Never lead with the solution — always lead with the pain ✓
- D) Use technical language to demonstrate expertise

**Why this matters:** When you lead with the solution ("Let me tell you about our content pipeline"), you're a vendor. When you lead with the pain ("You mentioned you haven't posted in two months — what's that costing you?"), you're an advisor. Advisors close at 3x the rate of vendors because the prospect feels understood, not sold to.

---

### Question 14
A prospect says their revenue has plateaued for 2-3 years. According to the pain mapping, this is usually what kind of problem?

- A) A marketing problem
- B) A hiring problem
- C) A capacity problem that systems can solve ✓
- D) A pricing problem

**Why this matters:** Revenue plateaus are almost always capacity ceilings, not demand ceilings. The business can't take on more work without more people, but can't afford more people without more revenue. AI OS breaks the catch-22 by adding capacity without adding headcount. This reframe — from "I need more leads" to "I need more capacity" — is a powerful insight to share (Challenger Sale: Teach).

---

### Question 15
When responding to a prospect's pain point, you should reference:

- A) The technical specifications of the AI OS module
- B) A list of all features included in the package
- C) The specific outcome in terms they care about (time saved, revenue gained, problems eliminated) ✓
- D) Your company's awards and certifications

**Why this matters:** Prospects buy outcomes, not features. "Our n8n workflow automates lead routing via webhook triggers" means nothing to a plumbing company owner. "Every lead gets a personalized follow-up in under 5 minutes, automatically" means everything. Speak their language, not yours.

## Section 4: Disqualification (Questions 16-20)

### Question 16
Which of the following is a HARD disqualifier (immediate stop)?

- A) The prospect wants to DIY implementation
- B) The prospect asks about pricing first
- C) The prospect has under $500K in revenue ✓
- D) The prospect hasn't responded to one follow-up

**Why this matters:** Hard disqualifiers save you from investing hours in prospects who cannot become successful clients. Under $500K, the budget isn't there and the ROI math doesn't work. This isn't a judgment on the business — it's a recognition that our product isn't designed for that stage. Being clear on this prevents wasted time for both sides.

---

### Question 17
A prospect says "I need to think about it" for the third time. What should you do?

- A) Send another proposal with more details
- B) Offer a discount to create urgency
- C) Ask directly what specifically they're unsure about, and be prepared to walk away ✓
- D) Give them more time and follow up in 30 days

**Why this matters:** "I need to think about it" said three times means they've already decided — and the answer is no. They just don't want to say it. Asking directly ("What specifically is holding you back?") gives them permission to voice the real objection. If they can't articulate one, the deal is dead. Accept it gracefully and move on.

---

### Question 18
What is the most important thing to do when declining a prospect who isn't a fit?

- A) Explain in detail why they don't qualify
- B) Offer a reduced-price alternative
- C) Add genuine value on the way out (recommendation, referral, helpful resource) ✓
- D) Stop responding to their messages

**Why this matters:** A graceful decline with a helpful recommendation does three things: earns respect, generates referrals, and keeps the door open. The prospect you decline today with class becomes the referral source who sends you a perfect-fit client next month. Your reputation is built as much by who you turn away as who you take on.

---

### Question 19
According to the time audit, what percentage of time does the average salesperson spend on prospects who will never buy?

- A) 25%
- B) 40%
- C) 65% ✓
- D) 80%

**Why this matters:** This number should alarm you. Nearly two-thirds of the average salesperson's time is wasted on dead ends. If you work 40 hours a week on sales, that's 26 hours going nowhere. Disqualification is not a nice-to-have skill — it's the difference between hitting quota and missing it. Redirecting even 10 of those wasted hours to qualified prospects changes your entire year.

---

### Question 20
A prospect compares your AI OS to hiring someone on Fiverr. What does this signal?

- A) They're price-sensitive but could be a good client
- B) They see AI OS as a commodity service and don't understand the value ✓
- C) They've done thorough market research
- D) They're ready to buy but negotiating

**Why this matters:** The Fiverr comparison means they think this is a one-off project — like getting a logo or a blog post. They don't understand that AI OS is an integrated operating system built for their specific business. Your job is to reframe: "That's like comparing a handyman to a general contractor. We're building the entire operational infrastructure." If they still can't see the difference after the reframe, they're not your buyer. Move on.
