## Part 2: Write 3 Personalized Outreach Messages (45-60 minutes)

Choose 3 of your 5 prospects and write a personalized outreach message for each. This is a first-touch message — NOT a pitch. The goal is to start a conversation, not close a deal.

**Outreach requirements:**
- Platform: LinkedIn connection request note (300 char limit) OR LinkedIn DM OR email
- Tone: Professional, curious, human. Not salesy.
- Must reference something specific about their business (not generic)
- Must include a value-add or insight (not just "I'd love to chat")
- Must end with a low-pressure call to action
- Must NOT mention pricing, packages, or "AI OS" by name

**What makes great outreach:**

Good outreach feels like it was written by someone who spent 10 minutes researching their business — because it was. It references something specific (a recent LinkedIn post, a Google review, their website, a news article). It offers value or insight. And it makes the prospect think: "This person actually understands my world."

**What makes bad outreach:**

Bad outreach is generic. It could be sent to 500 people without changing a word. "I help businesses grow using AI" is bad. "I noticed your Google reviews mention long wait times for callbacks — we helped a plumbing company similar to yours cut response time to under 2 minutes" is good.

## How Corey Will Evaluate

Corey reviews every submission personally. Here's what he's looking for:

**Research quality:** Did you actually dig into these businesses, or did you grab the first 5 Google results? The depth of your research signals the depth of your preparation on real sales calls.

**Pain identification:** Can you spot pain from the outside? This skill translates directly to discovery calls. If you can identify a prospect's pain before you talk to them, imagine how much more effective your questions will be.

**Outreach quality:** Would this message get a response? Corey has sent thousands of outreach messages and knows what works. He's looking for specificity, value, and a human voice.

**ICP discipline:** Did you target the right businesses, or did you default to whoever was easiest to find? Discipline in prospecting is discipline in sales.
