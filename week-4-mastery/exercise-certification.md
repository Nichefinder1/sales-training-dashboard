## Component 2: 60-Second Pitch

### The Assignment
Record a 60-second (maximum 90 seconds) video of yourself delivering the general AI OS pitch. Imagine you're at a networking event and someone asks: "So what does your company do?"

### What to Include
- Who Nichefinders AI is (1 sentence)
- What the AI OS does for business owners (2-3 sentences)
- One specific result (case study reference with numbers)
- Clear call to action ("If you know anyone who..." or "I'd love to learn more about your business...")

### Grading Rubric

| Criteria | 1 Point | 3 Points | 5 Points |
|----------|---------|----------|----------|
| **Confidence** | Hesitant, reads from notes, lots of filler words | Mostly confident with occasional hesitation | Natural, conversational, owns the room |
| **Clarity** | Confusing, prospect wouldn't understand | Mostly clear with some jargon or vague points | Crystal clear — a 12-year-old could understand |
| **Specificity** | Generic claims ("we help businesses grow") | Some specific numbers or examples | Specific numbers, named clients, concrete outcomes |
| **Call to Action** | No clear next step | Vague CTA ("let me know if you're interested") | Specific, natural CTA ("Can I grab your card?") |

**Scoring:**
- 20/20: Elite — you could pitch at a conference
- 16-19: Certified — you're ready for live conversations
- 12-15: Almost — refine and re-record
- Below 12: Need more practice — review Week 1 module on the 60-second pitch

**Passing Score: 16/20**

### Tips
- Practice 10+ times before recording
- Film in a well-lit, quiet space
- Look at the camera, not at notes
- Smile — enthusiasm is contagious
- Time yourself — 60 seconds goes fast
- Don't try to say everything — say the most important things with conviction

### Submission
Send the video to Corey via text, email, or Slack. He'll score it and provide feedback within 48 hours.

## Component 3: Mock Discovery Call

### The Assignment
Complete a 15-minute recorded role-play where Corey plays a prospect and you run a discovery call from start to finish.

### The Setup
- Corey will give you a prospect persona 24 hours in advance (industry, company size, rough pain points)
- You'll have time to research and prepare
- The call will be recorded (audio or video)
- Corey will behave like a real prospect — including objections, tangents, and testing your ability to redirect

### What You're Being Evaluated On

| Criteria | 1 Point | 3 Points | 5 Points |
|----------|---------|----------|----------|
| **Rapport Building** | Jumps straight to questions, no warmth | Some small talk but feels forced | Natural, genuine connection established in first 2 minutes |
| **Discovery Questions** | Reads from a list, surface-level | Good questions but doesn't dig deeper on key answers | Deep, adaptive questions that uncover real pain; follows up on key points |
| **Solution Alignment** | Generic pitch, doesn't connect to prospect's situation | Connects some features to some pain points | Every solution point directly addresses a stated pain with specific examples |
| **Objection Handling** | Freezes, argues, or gives up | Handles 1-2 objections but misses the real concern | Handles objections with empathy, labels the emotion, and redirects effectively |
| **Next Step Close** | Call ends without a clear next step | Mentions follow-up but no specific date/time | Clear, assumptive close with specific next step and date on the calendar |

**Scoring:**
- 25/25: Perfect — you could run this call unsupervised tomorrow
- 20-24: Certified — you're ready with Corey's support on closes
- 15-19: Almost — schedule another practice session
- Below 15: Need more preparation — review Weeks 2-4 materials

**Passing Score: 20/25**

### How to Prepare
1. Review the discovery call guide (`templates/discovery-call-guide.md`)
2. Practice with the role-play scenarios in `practice-scenarios/`
3. Record yourself doing a practice call with a friend
4. Time yourself — the full call should flow naturally within 15 minutes
5. Prepare for objections — Corey WILL throw at least 2-3 at you

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Talking more than 40% of the time (the prospect should talk 60%+)
- Asking questions but not listening to the answers
- Jumping to solutions before fully understanding the problem
- Forgetting to set a specific next step at the end
- Panicking when an objection comes up (pause, breathe, respond)

### Scheduling
Coordinate with Corey to schedule your mock call. Give yourself at least 24 hours of prep time after receiving the persona.

## Component 4: Prospect List

### The Assignment
Deliver a written prospect list of 10 researched, qualified prospects with personalized outreach messages for 5 of them.

### Requirements

**For all 10 prospects:**

| Field | Required |
|-------|----------|
| Company name | Yes |
| Industry | Yes |
| Location | Yes |
| Estimated revenue or employee count | Yes |
| Primary contact name and title | Yes |
| Contact email or LinkedIn URL | Yes |
| Pain point hypothesis (2-3 sentences) | Yes |
| Why they're a fit for AI OS | Yes |
| Source (how you found them) | Yes |

**For 5 of the 10, also include:**

| Field | Required |
|-------|----------|
| Personalized outreach message (email or LinkedIn) | Yes |
| Subject line (if email) | Yes |
| Specific hook (what you noticed about their business) | Yes |
| Proposed next step in the message | Yes |

### Qualification Criteria

Each prospect must meet at least 3 of these 5 criteria:
1. Revenue $1M+ or 10+ employees
2. Local service business or B2B company
3. Visible online pain point (weak Google presence, no reviews, outdated website)
4. Owner-operated (you can reach the decision maker)
5. Industry where AI OS delivers clear ROI (trades, professional services, agencies, nonprofits)

### Grading

This is pass/fail. To pass:
- All 10 prospects are real businesses (Corey will verify)
- All required fields are complete and accurate
- Pain point hypotheses are specific (not generic)
- Outreach messages are personalized (not templates with names swapped)
- At least 8/10 prospects meet qualification criteria

### Submission
Submit as a document (Google Doc, PDF, or Markdown) to Corey. He'll review and provide feedback within 48 hours.

### The Assignment
Set up your Airtable pipeline exactly as specified in Module 4.3 and enter your first 5 prospects.

### Requirements

**Airtable table must include these fields:**
- Company
- Contact Name
- Contact Email
- Contact Phone
- Stage (single select with all 9 stages)
- Source
- Last Contact
- Next Action
- Next Action Date
- Deal Size
- Temperature (Hot/Warm/Cold)
- Industry
- Employee Count
- Annual Revenue
- Pain Points
- Notes
- Created Date

**Views that must exist:**
- All Prospects
- Active Pipeline
- Follow-Up Due
- Hot Prospects
- Stale Deals
- Won Deals
- Lost Deals
- Nurture

**Data entry:**
- At least 5 prospects entered with all fields populated
- Each prospect must have a Next Action and Next Action Date
- At least 2 different pipeline stages represented

### Grading

Pass/fail. To pass:
- All fields exist with correct types
- All 8 views exist with correct filters
- 5+ prospects entered with complete data
- Next Action is specific (not "follow up" — "Send personalized email about GBP optimization")
- The pipeline makes sense (stages, temperatures, and notes are consistent)

### Submission
Share your Airtable base with Corey (viewer access) or screen-record a 2-minute walkthrough showing the fields, views, and data.

Most people pass on the first or second attempt. The training materials have everything you need — it's just a matter of practice and repetition.
